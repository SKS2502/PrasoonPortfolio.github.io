Experience

Experince-block-1

JULY 2020-MAY 2021

Final year thesis/project on Investing techniques to unify deep learning approaches for vision and non-vision tasks July 2020 - May 2021

I investigated the role of the state-of-the-art deep neural networks for vision tasks in solving non-vision tasks such as voice and speech recognition and Natural language and vice versa. The recent work on successfully using convolutional DNNs to solve speech recognition and transformers to solve object detection shows that these seemingly far problems in different domains do have commonality. My goal was to understand and evolve such techniques to unify deep learning tools to solve problems from different input modalities and domains. This project was a preparation towards what I would like to also continue further during my graduate research.

OCT 2020-DEC 2020

RESEARCH INTERNSHIP AT TCS OCT 2020 - DEC 2020

We built DigiFleet, a system that takes input from a variety of IoT sensors deployed in cars and other gasoline vehicles and predicts the carbon emission from the respective vehicles. My role was to develop a deep neural network model with such input modes affecting carbon emission such as fuel type, load of vehicle, weather condition, engine efficiency, age of vehicle etc. A python dashboard to visualize the output will also be built. I was experimenting with RNN because we were modeling a complex dataset and needed more prediction power and a flexible and extensible network as input modes evolve over time

2021

FIRST WORK IN PhD in Fall 2021

Investigated the robustness of the HyperDimensional Computing model by performing different types of adversarial and non-adversarial attacks. After this,I implemented the inference layer of HD computing with the classical computing which results in 10 percent increase of accuracy and 2.8 times faster than classical computing methods for the same data. Currently, I am trying to increase the efficiency of HD computing in the field of speech recognition. Currently I am working on the Uncertainty and Robustness of Deep learning models. I am using an unsupervised method Detco for image classification because recent pre-training methods are only designed for image classification. I will use this method along with other OOD detection technique called VOS(Virtual Outlier system) to find the uncertainty in the model and will try to reduce the calibration error. I am trying to prove that self-supervised methods give less calibration compared to state-of-the-art supervised methods.
